Short Bio

Before coming to Minnesota, I received my bachelor's degree in Applied Math from Huazhong University of Science and Technology. Now, I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in the Division of Biostatistics at the University of Minnesota. I am co-advised by Prof. Weihua Guan and Prof. Wei Pan. My research is supported by the Doctoral Dissertation Fellowship.

The long-term goal of my research is to develop new statistical methods and theory for high dimensional data. The specific research questions that I am trying to answer include:

  • How can we develop more powerful testing methods for high-dimensional data? Details

  • How can we solve many practical problems generated by high-dimensional genetic and genomic data, especially via some machine learning methods? Details

With this focus in mind, my research interests range from high dimensional data and machine learning to statistical genetics.
